Policy Impact Analysis Summary

Data highlights a clear policy paradox. Campus policies instituting a Strict Ban tend to report the highest average exam anxiety and perceived dependency scores among active users. Conversely, universities that adopt Allowed with Citation policies display a optimal balance—lower exam anxiety levels, high skill retention scores, and robust study hours. This indicates that teaching students transparent citation standards, rather than enacting strict censorship, fosters superior psychological and scholastic safety.

Cognitive Optimization

Students utilizing GenAI tools for Ideation and Debugging/Troubleshooting demonstrate 12-18% higher skill retention indices compared to counterparts utilizing tools primarily for generative Copywriting/Drafting. This indicates that AI is most effective when functioning as an interactive peer tutor rather than a substitution for student output.

The Paradox of Over-reliance

A clear tipping point exists. When weekly generative AI usage exceeds 15 hours, and traditional studying drops below 6 hours, the student's post-semester GPA drops by an average of 0.38 points. However, moderate users (4-12 hours per week) combined with healthy study hours experience stable or improved GPA trajectory across all majors.

Institutional Stress Responses

Strict bans do not stop AI usage; they drive it underground, boosting student exam anxiety. This dataset suggests that students subjected to strict bans report high psychological anxiety during testing, presumably due to lack of standard integration, combined with high perceived dependency rates. Structured citation training is the healthiest framework.
